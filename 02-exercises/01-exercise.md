### &#128313; Q1. What is the most appropriate measurement scale for university students' letter grades (A, B, C, D, F)?

<details>
<summary><strong>Show answer</strong></summary>

**Ordinal scale** — grades have a clear order (A > B > C > D > F), but the differences between grades are not numerically measurable.
</details>

---

### &#128313; Q2. What scale describes the level of urgency in a hospital emergency room (low, moderate, high, critical)?

<details>
<summary><strong>Show answer</strong></summary>

**Ordinal scale** — urgency levels are ranked from low to critical, but the intervals between levels are not measurable quantities.
</details>

---

### &#10067; Q3. What scale describes the classification of animals in a zoo (mammal, bird, reptile, ...)?

<details>
<summary><strong>Show answer</strong></summary>

**Nominal scale** — animal classes are categories with no natural ordering between them.
</details>

---

### &#10067; Q4. What scale describes carbon dioxide levels in the atmosphere (measured in ppm)?

<details>
<summary><strong>Show answer</strong></summary>

**Ratio scale** — CO&#8322; concentration is numeric, has a true zero (no CO&#8322;), and ratios are meaningful (400 ppm is twice as much as 200 ppm).
</details>

---

### &#128205; Q5. What scale describes the distance from a building to the center of campus?

<details>
<summary><strong>Show answer</strong></summary>

**Ratio scale** — distance is numeric, has a true zero, and ratios are meaningful (200 m is twice as far as 100 m).

**Recap — the 4 scales in order of increasing information:**
| Scale | Order? | Equal intervals? | True zero? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Nominal | No | No | No |
| Ordinal | Yes | No | No |
| Interval | Yes | Yes | No |
| Ratio | Yes | Yes | Yes |
</details>
